Premium priced generics Dashboard updates

View dashboard (opens in a new tab)

There's an increasing volume of computerised prescriptions each month where the prescriber has prescribed a generic Drug Tariff Part VIIIA product but also with the name of the branded product or the supplier/ manufacturer added in brackets.

In these situations, the drug costs charged to the prescriber's budget and reimbursed to the community pharmacy or dispensing practice will be that of the list price of the branded medicine or the specified supplier.

In many cases this cost will be significantly higher than the Drug Tariff Part VIIIA listed price.

To help SICBLs monitor this scenario more closely and explore opportunities for review, we've developed this dashboard to highlight the details of practices and drugs where this is most common.

Our method

Data includes current spend, potential spend and potential saving, these appear to be provided to us at SICBL level but calculated elsewhere in the dashboard. Potential saving is calculated, detail included in the dictionary.

  • Gareth Gubbage (Product Owner)

Time period

January 2014 to March 2025

Geographic coverage

Limited to organisations held as GP practices in our organisational database.

Organisational level

Data is available at both practice level and Sub-Integrated Care Board Location (SICBL) level.

How often is the data updated

Updated monthly, in line with the ePACT2 refresh (usually around the 20th of each month). Includes prescribing of generic-name AMPs (generics with a supplier name) only where a saving is possible.

Data lineage/provenance

ePACT2 is the source of most of the data.

No patient information is included in the dashboard. Data is aggregated to practice level. Risk of reidentification of patients is low. Purpose is a cost analysis for NHS organisations. To be used by appropriate NHS professionals.

Caveats and limitations

Data is limited to GP practices and where we have calculated that there is a potential saving based on the costs of the products prescribed. Comparison of cost appears to be the current price vs the price in the month specified. The data may not be representative of recent changes in price. No suppression has been applied, access to the dashboard is limited to registered users.
